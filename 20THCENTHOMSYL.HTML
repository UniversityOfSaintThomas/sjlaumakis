COURSE DESCRIPTION/GOALS:

This course is an examination of the influence of the thought of Thomas Aquinas on prominent Thomistic philosophers and theologians of the 20th century. These thinkers include: Pierre Rousselot, Joseph Marechal, Jacques Maritain, Etienne Gilson, Karl Rahner, and Bernard Lonergan.

The aim of the course is to study both the intellectual influence and historical development of the thought of Thomas Aquinas in his philosophical and theological successors. The versions of Thomism to be studied include: Aristotelian Thomism, Existential Thomism, Historical Thomism, Platonic Thomism, Transcendental Thomism, and Analytical Thomism. It is expected by the end of the semester that you both know something of the thought of the major Thomistic philosophers and theologians of the 20th century and be able to articulate coherently your own understanding of the evolution and development of 20th century Thomism.

ASSIGNMENTS:

Since this is an upper level course emphasizing the understanding and integration of complex and interrelated ideas, you are asked to concentrate on assimilating these philosophical concepts and notions. Reading of assigned texts is a MUST in order to introduce you to the subject matter, facilitate class discussion and participation, pose distressing questions, and just plain make you think. The course will begin with a lecture format (roughly 4 weeks) as we review the philosophy and theology of Thomas Aquinas as well as the history of philosophy in the Modern and Contemporary periods. It will then proceed in seminar style with SIGNIFICANT emphasis on student participation. There will be 2 PAPERS (One 2-3 typed pages in length and NOT MORE THAN 4 TYPED PAGES and One longer paper) on pre-assigned readings. The first or shorter paper will be presented to the class along with a one page outline. The longer paper will focus on the specific ideas of one of the 20th century Thomists. Late papers will be penalized one full grade for each day they are late. There will also be 2 EXAMS--a Mid-Term Exam and a Final Exam at the end of the semester.
