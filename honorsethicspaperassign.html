PAPER #1--THE ROLE OF TRADITION IN THE GOOD HUMAN LIFE

 

1. READ Soyinka's DEATH & THE KING'S HORSEMAN & Confucius' ANALECTS!

2. Notice how Soyinka & Confucius appeal to the notion of duty or obligation to a tradition--an ethical tradition--a past--that impacts on the present.

3. Begin your paper by specifying your own understanding of the notion of a moral/ethical tradition.

4. Explain how the notion of duty or obligation is related to tradition.

5. Note the strengths and weaknesses of such a tradition.

6. Conclude by arguing for or against such a tradition.

7. The point of this paper is for you to engage in a critical reflection on the strengths and weaknesses of appealing to a moral/ethical tradition as a guide for living a good human life. In other words, the purpose is to get you to think about the role of tradition as a guide to living a good human life.

PAPER #3--THE CASE FOR OR AGAINST ARTIFICIAL CONTRACEPTION

 

1. Read the arguments for and against artificial contraception!

2. Decide which argument(s) is/are better.

3. Present/Lay out what you take to be the best argument for or against artificial contraception.

4. Critically evaluate the argument--explain why its premises are true and why you accept it.

5. The point of this paper is for you to reflect on the arguments for and against artificial contraception and then present and defend what you take to be the best argument either for or against artificial contraception.

DIRECTIONS FOR WRITING PAPERS

 

 

1. TITLE PAGE/FIRST PAGE

*Title of paper in center/middle of page

*Your name in lower right hand corner

*Date of paper in lower right hand corner

*Paper number (i.e., Paper #1) in lower right hand corner

 

 

2. INTRODUCTION

*Begin by stating your objective/purpose

*(i.e., The purpose of this paper is ...)

*Say why you THINK this is an important topic/ question

*Explain what you are going to do/show in the paper

 

 

3. MAKE YOUR CASE

*Present your evidence/set up/lay out your argument(s)

*i.e., Plato thinks that Socrates is NOT a Sophist BECAUSE:

 

P1--If a Sophist, Then Lots of $$$

P2--~(Lots of $$$)

C--S0 ~ a Sophist

 

*REMEMBER--you are trying to justify what you believe

 

 

4. CRITICALLY EVALUATE THE ARGUMENT(S)

*State what kind of argument it is

*(i.e., Modus Ponens, Modus Tollens, Induction, Disjunction)

*Check its validity/strength

*Explain why its PREMISES are TRUE or FALSE

*Any counter-examples???

*Say what you THINK about the argument(s)

 

 

5. CONCLUSION

*Say what you have done/shown

*(i.e., This paper has shown that ...)
