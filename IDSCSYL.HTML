COURSE DESCRIPTION/GOALS:

In accord with the Renaissance Program's committment to foster the integration of theoretical and practical learning, the purpose of this course is to examine the connection between technology and the human condition. More specifically, the course will help students develop the philosophical ability to reflect on both practical and theoretical issues involving the nature and place of technology and its impact on the lives of human beings.

 

REQUIRED TEXTS:

FERRE, PHILOSOPHY OF TECHNOLOGY

MITCHAM & MACKEY, PHILOSOPHY AND TECHNOLOGY

MOKYR, THE LEVER OF RICHES

SELECTED HANDOUTS

 

ASSIGNMENTS:

Since this is a course relying upon concepts and models stemming from both theoretical and practical sources in an attempt to integrate aspects of these diverse branches of higher learning, you are asked to concentrate on learning these distinct but related notions. Reading of assigned texts is a MUST in order to introduce you to the subject matter, facilitate class discussion and participation, pose distressing questions, and just plain make you THINK. There will be 3 SHORT PAPERS (2-3 typed pages in length and NOT MORE THAN 4 TYPED PAGES) on pre-assigned topics and readings. One paper will be presented in class (with a one-page outline for each member of the class). There will also be at least 2 EXAMS/TESTS.
