COURSE DESCRIPTION:

This course will examine angels as objects of religious beliefs and as objects of philosophical thought. It will begin with a brief survey of some recent works on angels, and then proceed with a historical and philosophical investigation of angelic nature(s). Special attention will be given to the arguments for and against the existence of angels in the works of Pseudo-Dionysius, Aquinas, Descartes, Hobbes, Locke, MacGregor, and Adler.

 

REQUIRED TEXTS:

Adler, The Angels and Us

Aquinas, On Spiritual Creatures

Connolly, In Search of Angels

Danielou, The Angels and Their Mission

MacGregor, Angels--Ministers of Grace

Selected Handouts

 

ASSIGNMENTS:

Since this is a second level course emphasizing both the understanding and employment of elementary ideas, you are asked to concentrate on learning these fundamental philosophical notions. Reading of assigned texts is a MUST in order to introduce you to the subject matter, facilitate class discussion and participation, pose distressing questions, and just plain make you think. There will be at least 1 SHORT PAPER (2-3 typed pages in length and NOT MORE THAN 4 TYPED PAGES) on a pre-assigned reading, and 1 LONG PAPER (5-10 typed pages in length). At least 1 paper will be presented to the class along with a 1 page summary/outline for the other members of the class. There will also be at least 2 EXAMS.
