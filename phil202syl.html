COURSE DESCRIPTION/GOALS:

This course in the history of philosophy examines major themes and philosophers of the Medieval period (400-1400). It will trace the historical development of philosophical thought by highlighting the fundamental ideas in the thought of several important thinkers (i.e., Augustine, Boethius, Anselm, Bonaventure and Aquinas). The aim of the course is to introduce you to Medieval thought by presenting the questions that Medieval philosophers thought about (i.e., the relationship of faith & reason, the existence of God, the problem of universals, and the nature of mystical experience) and by exploring their answers to them. It is expected by the end of the semester that you know something of the thought of several major thinkers and appreciate the rich and complex history of Medieval thought.

ASSIGNMENTS:

Since this is a foundation-type course emphasizing the understanding of elementary ideas, you are asked to concentrate on learning these fundamental philosophical notions. Reading of assigned texts is a MUST in order to introduce you to the subject matter, facilitate class discussion and participation, pose distressing questions, and just plain make you think. There will be at least 2 SHORT PAPERS (2-3 typed pages in length and NOT MORE THAN 4 TYPED PAGES) on pre-assigned readings. At least 1 paper will be presented to the class along with a 1-page summary/outline for the other members of the class. There will also be at least 2 EXAMS.
